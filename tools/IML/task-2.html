§ 01 — DESIGN RATIONALE
Shape-First Accessibility Principle
All Risk Tier icons are designed to be distinguishable by shape and metaphor alone — not relying on color as the primary differentiator. This ensures full accessibility for users with deuteranopia, protanopia, and other color vision deficiencies. Color is used as a secondary reinforcement layer, not the sole encoding method. Each icon uses a distinct geometric metaphor: a shield for Low Risk (protection), a warning triangle for Medium Risk (caution), and a flame shape for High Risk (danger). Sync states use universally understood iconography — a cloud with arrow for save, and rotating arrows for progress.
